Current Affairs

The Uniform Civil Code, or UCC, is a topic that frequently sparks debate in India, raising important questions about equality, cultural identity, and the very fabric of our secular democracy. The core idea behind a Uniform Civil Code is to create a common set of laws governing personal matters like marriage, divorce, inheritance, and adoption, applicable to all citizens regardless of religion. This would replace the existing system, where different communities follow their own personal laws.

On one hand, a UCC promises equality before the law. Today, different laws for different groups can lead to inequality, particularly in areas like women’s rights and inheritance. A Uniform Civil Code could bring consistency, ensuring that everyone, irrespective of religion or community, is subject to the same legal principles.
